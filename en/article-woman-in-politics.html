Marlene Coulomb-Gully. Women in politics

Who is Marlene Coulomb-Gully ?

Marlene Coulomb-Gully was born on May 14, 1958, in Thionville (Moselle). She is a French researcher in political communication and on the representation of gender in the media. She also teaches information and communication science at the University of Toulouse.

Marlene coulomb-Gully wants equality between women and men. In 2013, she was appointed to the high council for equality between women and men. She was appointed because of her research work on gender equality. Her term was renewed in 2016. In 2020, she joined the equality observatory of the superior Audiovisual Council (CSA), and in January 2017 she was named Knight of the Legion of Honor.

Women in politics

Marlene Coulomb-Gully tries to show that women in politics are not very present. She says that the number of women in very high position is very rare. Moreover, now there is a law that obliges the equality of the number of people participating in the political event. This law is called : " the law of parity " voted in 2000. This law requires the equality of the sexes to participate in an election. That is to say that if 5 men present themselves, it is necessary to also have 5 women who present themselves too.

This law works very well in different area but not all. We know today that the municipal councils of cities with more than 1000 inhabitants have a perfect parity. The departmental and regional councils also have perfect parity. But the law of parity is not respected in all political fields. For example, during the vote of the Senate or the National Assembly, the number of women present is not the same as men.

We also know that in the history of the 5th Republic, there has never been a woman president in France, neither president of the Senate nor president of the National Assembly.

In France, there is only one-woman prime minister out of several dozen. It is Edith Cresson who was Prime Minister from May 1991 to April 1992, so about 10 months. But she didn't take good decisions, and she wasn't very popular.

There are very few women presidents of regional and departmental councils. There are also few women mayors. We can see that there is a sharing of places in a certain number of political bodies but there is not yet a real sharing of power.
